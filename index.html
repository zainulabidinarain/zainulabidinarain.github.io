Research & Publications

Focused on non-destructive evaluation, thermographic imaging, and deep learning for industrial inspection.

Published IJMCP, Vol. 2(1)

Mitigation of DDoS Attacks with Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Transformers

Zain Ul Abidin, Haseeb, Muhammad Younis, Habeeban Memon, Junaid Ahmed Bhatti

Evaluates ML/DL models - including LightGBM and XGBoost - for multi-class DDoS detection using the CICDDoS2019 dataset. Tree-based models achieved highest accuracy with fast execution, making them suitable for real-time cybersecurity applications.

DOI: 10.61503/Ijmcp.v2i1.181 ↗
Under Review Nondestructive Testing & Evaluation · Taylor & Francis

Metal Defect Detection in Pulsed Thermographic Imaging via Temporal Eigen Projection and Robust Low-Rank Tensor Completion Model

Zain Ul Abidin, Junaid Ahmed, Dong Wang, Guiyun Tian

Introduces the "Eigen-RHOTC" framework to identify surface cracks in metallic parts using Eddy Current Pulsed Thermography (ECPT). Combines temporal eigen projection with robust higher-order tensor completion, significantly outperforming existing methods in SNR and computational speed. Submission ID: 255311532.

Under Review Quantitative InfraRed Thermography Journal · Taylor & Francis

Eddy Current Pulsed Thermography Based Efficient Defect Detection in Metals via Keyframe Extraction and Accelerated Sparse Decomposition

Junaid Ahmed, Zain Ul Abidin, Guiyun Tian, Dong Wang

Proposes a rapid defect detection methodology using entropy and density clustering for keyframe extraction, significantly reducing processing load, with accelerated CUR decomposition for isolating weak defect signals from noise. Submission ID: 254751271.

Accepted · IEEE Xplore iCoMET Conference

Debond Detection in CFRP Structures using Optical Pulse Thermography and Deep Learning

Habeeban Memon, Zain Ul Abidin, Junaid Ahmed, Ayesha Siddiqua

Evaluates YOLOv8, Faster R-CNN, and Mask R-CNN for automated debond detection in CFRP composites. YOLOv8 is optimal for rapid screening; Mask R-CNN achieves highest accuracy with pixel-wise defect segmentation. Indexed in EI Compendex.

Under Review Image and Vision Computing · Elsevier

Improving Image Super-Resolution: A Hybrid Approach Using DT-CWT and Transformer Networks

Dr. Pan Zhiwen, Faisal Sahito, Aakash Kumar, Dr. Junaid Ahmed, Zain Ul Abidin

Presents a hybrid deep learning architecture combining Dual-Tree Complex Wavelet Transform (DT-CWT) with Transformer networks to significantly enhance image super-resolution capabilities and high-frequency detail retention.

In Preparation Manuscript Writing

Automated Defect Detection in CFRP Composites Using Multi-Modal Thermal Representations and Deep Learning

Zain Ul Abidin, Habeeban Memon, Junaid Ahmed, Ayesha Siddiqua

Introduces a three-channel thermal representation embedding raw spatial intensity, PCA variance structure, and thermographic signal reconstruction dynamics into unified tensors. Achieves up to 154% relative gains in segmentation accuracy with physics-informed depth estimation for 3D defect reconstruction.
