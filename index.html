Research and Publications

Focused on non-destructive evaluation, thermographic imaging, and deep learning for industrial inspection.

Published IJMCP, Vol. 2(1), 2024

Mitigation of DDoS Attacks with Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Transformers

Zain Ul Abidin, Haseeb, Muhammad Younis, Habeeban Memon, Junaid Ahmed Bhatti

Benchmarked ML and DL models for multi-class DDoS detection on CICDDoS2019. LightGBM and XGBoost achieved the best accuracy-speed balance, making them well-suited for real-time network security applications.

DOI: 10.61503/Ijmcp.v2i1.181 ↗
Accepted · IEEE Xplore iCoMET 2025 · Scopus (EI Compendex)

Debond Detection in CFRP Structures Using Optical Pulse Thermography and Deep Learning

Habeeban Memon, Zain Ul Abidin, Junaid Ahmed, Ayesha Siddiqua

Compared YOLOv8, Faster R-CNN, and Mask R-CNN for CFRP debond detection from optical pulse thermography image sequences. Mask R-CNN achieved the highest accuracy with pixel-level defect segmentation, while YOLOv8 is optimal for rapid screening.

Preprint on ResearchGate ↗
Under Review Nondestructive Testing and Evaluation · Taylor and Francis · IF 4.2 · Q1

Metal Defect Detection in Pulsed Thermographic Imaging via Temporal Eigen Projection and Robust Low-Rank Tensor Completion Model

Zain Ul Abidin, Junaid Ahmed, Dong Wang, Guiyun Tian

Proposes the Eigen-RHOTC framework combining temporal eigen projection with robust higher-order tensor completion to isolate ECPT defect features in metallic structures. Outperforms prior methods in SNR and computational speed.

Preprint on ResearchGate ↗
Under Review Quantitative InfraRed Thermography Journal · Taylor and Francis · IF 4.9 · Q1

Eddy Current Pulsed Thermography based Efficient Defect Detection in Metals via Keyframe Extraction and Accelerated Sparse Decomposition

Junaid Ahmed, Zain Ul Abidin, Guiyun Tian, Dong Wang

Entropy- and density-based keyframe selection reduces thermal data volume. Accelerated CUR decomposition extracts weak defect signals from noisy backgrounds.

Preprint on ResearchGate ↗

Improving Image Super-Resolution: A Hybrid Approach Using DT-CWT and Transformer Networks

Pan Zhiwen, Faisal Sahito, Aakash Kumar, Junaid Ahmed, Zain Ul Abidin

A DT-CWT and Transformer hybrid for image super-resolution that preserves fine structural detail better than current state-of-the-art baselines.

Zain Ul Abidin, Habeeban Memon, Junaid Ahmed, Ayesha Siddiqua
